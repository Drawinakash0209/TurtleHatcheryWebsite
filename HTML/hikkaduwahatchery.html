Hikkaduwa
TURTLE
hatchery

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Center, which aims to preserve some of the world's endangered turtle species, is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town. The southern shore of the island is home to a number of turtle hatcheries, including this one. It began as a community initiative to help safeguard the southern coast's biodiversity, but it has since developed into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue facility with devoted personnel that toil around the clock to study, look after, and safeguard various turtle species.

The hatchery and rescue facility currently cares for five different species of turtles. These species include the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle. Modern water tanks are included in the facilities at the sea turtle hatchery and rescue center, which also cares for adult turtle species that have been pulled from the water after suffering injuries. The place where the turtles lay their eggs, which has distinct small mounds of sand piled neatly between one another, is one of the more odd sights in the center. The turtle eggs' hatching process is carefully tracked at each of these mounds.

What can you do here

If you go to the Turtle Hatchery, there are loads of enjoyable activities. You can go to Hikkaduwa National Park, Hikkaduwa Beach, learn about turtles, go sightseeing, snap pictures, and much more.

About US

Thank you for visiting the Sri Lanka Turtle Hatchery website, a space dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles. Protecting these amazing species and their habitats is our main priority. Our organization was founded with the intention of ensuring the welfare and survival of sea turtle species that are in risk of extinction, and our objective is to create a safe haven where these ancient marine reptiles can thrive.

Our Mission

Our goal is to raise awareness about the value of turtle conservation, promote sustainable practices, and inculcate a sense of responsibility for these fascinating creatures. Join us as we work to ensure that sea turtles can live gracefully in our waters for a long time to come.
